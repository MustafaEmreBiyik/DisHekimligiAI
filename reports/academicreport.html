Oral Lichen Planus (Erosive / Reticular Pattern)

case_id: olp_001 Difficulty: Advanced Category: Immunologic / Mucosal ⚠ OPMD
Patient

Eleanor Vance · Female · 58 years old
High School Administrator

Chief Complaint (Initial Presentation)

"My gums are incredibly raw and they burn constantly, especially when I eat anything spicy, salty, or acidic."

Patient Psychology / Unreliable Narrator Mechanics
Baseline State & Communication Style
Anxious and exhausted by chronic discomfort. Polite but evasive regarding her full medical history. She firmly believes her problem is a local allergic reaction to a new whitening toothpaste or excessively hot coffee. She has moderate health literacy but dismisses OTC supplements and pain relievers as "non-medical" and irrelevant to dental care.
Disclosed Medical History (Volunteered)

• Hyperlipidaemia — Atorvastatin 20 mg daily
• Mild Osteoarthritis (hands and knees)

Hidden Medical History (Requires Direct Questioning)

• Naproxen sodium (OTC, self-medicating for arthritis pain)
• Ibuprofen (occasional, believes it is not "a real medication")

Physical Examination Nodes
🔎
Trigger: perform_extraoral_exam → Skin inspection (wrists & ankles)

Several small, violaceous, flat-topped, pruritic polygonal papules observed on the flexor surfaces of the wrists and ankles — pathognomonic cutaneous lichen planus, confirming the systemic autoimmune nature of the condition.

🦷
Trigger: perform_intraoral_exam → Buccal Mucosa

Bilateral fine, white, lace-like network of intersecting lines (Wickham's striae) on the posterior buccal mucosa, superimposed over a slightly erythematous base.

🩸
Trigger: perform_intraoral_exam → Gingiva

Attached gingiva of maxillary and mandibular anterior quadrants: diffuse, bright red, raw-appearing patches. Mucosa appears thinned, glazed, and without stippling — consistent with Desquamative Gingivitis.

🧻
Trigger: perform_gauze_wipe_test (Clinical Distinction Test)

Firmly wiping the white lace-like striae with dry gauze does not remove the white network. It remains firmly fixed to the underlying epithelium. This eliminates pseudomembranous candidiasis from the differential diagnosis.

🔬
Trigger: perform_nikolsky_test (Clinical Distinction Test)

Lateral shearing pressure on normal-appearing adjacent mucosa does not induce bulla formation or epithelial separation. Nikolsky sign: Negative. This differentiates OLP from Mucous Membrane Pemphigoid.

Diagnostic Testing — Results Revealed by Student Actions
Trigger: order_incisional_biopsy → H&E Histopathology
Hyperparakeratosis; thickening of the granular cell layer; characteristic saw-toothed rete pegs; prominent liquefaction degeneration of the basal cell layer; dense, band-like T-lymphocytic infiltrate in the superficial lamina propria; multiple Civatte bodies (apoptotic keratinocytes). No evidence of epithelial dysplasia or invasive SCC.
Trigger: order_incisional_biopsy → Direct Immunofluorescence (DIF)
Positive: continuous, shaggy, linear fibrinogen band at the basement membrane zone. Negative: for linear or intercellular deposits of IgG, IgA, IgM, and C3. (This pattern is characteristic of OLP and distinguishes it from Mucous Membrane Pemphigoid, which shows linear IgG + C3.)
Serology (Trigger: request_serology)

HCV antibody: Negative.
Fasting blood glucose: 95 mg/dL (Normal).

Correct Diagnosis

Oral Lichen Planus — Erosive & Reticular pattern (OPMD classification).
Differential: OLP vs Lichenoid Drug Reaction (NSAIDs — Naproxen), Candidiasis, MMP.

Correct Treatment

Topical corticosteroid (e.g., Fluocinonide gel) to erosive lesions — only after biopsy confirms diagnosis. Candidiasis prophylaxis/monitoring. Suspect and investigate NSAID lichenoid reaction.

Maximum Score Potential

~120 pts across history (20 pts), extraoral exam (10), intraoral exam (20), clinical tests (25+25), biopsy (30), correct diagnosis (40).

🔎 Key Mistake Summary (For Paper Write-Up)

  • Mistake: Prescribed topical immunosuppressive corticosteroid (Fluocinonide) immediately after observing white mucosal lesions, without first ordering a biopsy.
  • Rule triggered: prescribe_corticosteroid + prerequisite order_incisional_biopsy → Enforcement: BLOCK_AND_PENALIZE
  • Penalty: −50 pts (most severe rule in the system).
  • Clinical rationale: OPMD classification mandates histopathological dysplasia screening before immunosuppression. Masking a malignancy with steroids is a patient safety catastrophe.
  • Pedagogical value: Trains students in the essential OPMD protocol: Observe → Clinically test → Biopsy → Diagnose → Treat. The severity of the penalty (−50 vs the standard −20) reflects the real-world stakes of this specific error class.
